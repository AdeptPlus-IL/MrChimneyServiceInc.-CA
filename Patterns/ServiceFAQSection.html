Common Questions

Chimney Sweeping FAQs

Questions before you book? Here are the ones we hear most often.

How often should I have my chimney swept?

The CSIA recommends annual sweeping for chimneys used regularly. In Los Angeles, where fireplaces often go unused for extended periods, we recommend at minimum one inspection and cleaning per year — even if use has been light. Debris and moisture damage can occur regardless of how often you burn.

How long does a chimney sweep take?

Most standard sweeping appointments take between 45 minutes and 1.5 hours, depending on the condition of the chimney and the level of buildup present. We'll give you a more accurate estimate when you call.

Will the sweep make a mess in my home?

No. Our technicians use drop cloths, vacuum systems, and containment equipment to keep your home clean throughout the process. We clean up completely before we leave.

Do I need to be home during the sweep?

Yes — an adult homeowner or authorized representative needs to be present for the appointment.

What's the difference between a sweep and an inspection?

A sweep removes buildup and debris from the flue. An inspection assesses the structural condition of the chimney system — the liner, crown, damper, firebox, and exterior masonry. We recommend combining both for a complete picture of your chimney's health.

What if you find a problem during the sweep?

We'll document it and explain it to you clearly before we leave. We'll never perform additional work without your approval. If a repair is needed, we'll give you an honest assessment and a straightforward quote.
