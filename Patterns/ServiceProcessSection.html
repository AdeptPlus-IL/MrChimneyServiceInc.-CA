Our Sweeping Process

What We Remove — and How

Not all chimney buildup is the same. Creosote forms in three distinct stages, each requiring a different approach. Our technicians are trained to identify and remove all types of buildup safely and thoroughly.

Stage 1 Creosote

Light, flaky deposits that form during low-temperature burns. The easiest to remove and the most common result of regular fireplace use. Standard brushing clears it completely.

Stage 2 Creosote

Hardened, tar-like deposits caused by cooler flue temperatures and smoldering fires. Requires more aggressive brushing techniques and specialized tools to fully clear.

Stage 3 Creosote

Glazed, concentrated buildup that coats the flue lining. The most hazardous and most difficult to remove — often requires chemical treatment or relining. We'll advise on the right approach.

Soot

Fine black powder that settles throughout the flue and firebox. Removed during every standard sweep.

Debris & Blockages

Leaves, twigs, and animal nesting materials that accumulate in unused chimneys. We clear the full flue from top to bottom.

Moisture Deposits

Staining and mineral residue caused by water intrusion. We document any signs of moisture damage during every sweep.
