Common Questions

Chimney Sweeping FAQs

Questions before you book? Here are the ones we hear most often.

How often should my chimney be swept?

The National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) recommends annual chimney inspections and sweeping as needed based on creosote buildup. If you use your fireplace frequently, annual sweeping is typically recommended. Even if you use your chimney infrequently, an annual inspection is important to identify any issues.

What is creosote and why does it need to be removed?

Creosote is a flammable byproduct of burning wood that accumulates on the interior walls of your chimney. It comes in three levels of buildup, from light powder to thick, tar-like substance. Creosote buildup significantly increases the risk of chimney fires, which can cause extensive damage and pose serious safety hazards to your home.

What happens during a chimney sweeping?

Our technician will inspect your chimney system, use professional equipment to remove soot and creosote buildup from the interior walls, clear any debris or obstructions, and provide you with a detailed report of findings and recommendations. We protect your home throughout the process to minimize any mess.

Will chimney sweeping make a mess in my home?

Professional chimney sweeping does generate dust and debris, but we take precautions to minimize mess. We use protective coverings and proper equipment to contain debris, and we thoroughly clean the fireplace area when finished.

How long does a chimney sweeping appointment take?

Most chimney sweeping appointments take 1-2 hours, depending on the condition of your chimney and the extent of buildup. We'll provide you with an estimated timeframe during your free estimate.

What are signs my chimney needs to be swept?

Common signs include: smoke backing up into your home, visible soot around the fireplace, unpleasant odors from the chimney, reduced fireplace efficiency, or evidence of animals or nests. An annual inspection is the best way to catch issues early.

Can a dirty chimney cause a fire?

Yes. Creosote buildup is highly flammable and is the leading cause of chimney fires. These fires can reach extremely high temperatures and cause serious damage to your chimney structure and potentially spread to your home.

Do gas fireplace chimneys need to be swept?

Gas fireplaces produce less creosote than wood-burning fireplaces, but they still require regular inspection and occasional cleaning to ensure proper operation and remove any debris or buildup.

Is chimney sweeping necessary if I don't use my fireplace often?

Yes. Even if your fireplace is used infrequently, an annual inspection is important. Chimneys can develop problems over time, including animal nests, debris accumulation, or structural issues that need attention regardless of usage frequency.

What if animals or nests are in my chimney?

We can identify animal nests or obstructions during our inspection. While chimney sweeping removes some debris, animal removal may require additional specialized services. We'll advise you of the best course of action and can connect you with appropriate solutions.
