Inspection Services

Three Levels of Chimney Inspection

Every chimney is different. We follow NFPA 211 standards to determine which inspection level your home needs — no guesswork, no unnecessary upsells.

Level 1

Annual Safety Check

A visual examination of the readily accessible portions of your chimney and fireplace. Recommended annually for systems under regular use with no changes or known problems.

  • ✓ Exterior & interior visual check
  • ✓ Flue blockage assessment
  • ✓ Creosote buildup evaluation
Level 2

Real Estate & Change of Use

Includes everything in Level 1 plus video scanning of the flue interior. Required when buying or selling a home, after storms, or when changing fuel types.

  • ✓ Full Level 1 inspection
  • ✓ Video camera flue scan
  • ✓ Accessible attic & crawl space check
Level 3

Hazard Investigation

The most comprehensive inspection, involving removal of components to access concealed areas. Required when a serious hazard is suspected, such as a chimney fire or structural failure.

  • ✓ Full Level 1 & 2 inspection
  • ✓ Concealed area access
  • ✓ Structural integrity assessment
Real Estate Inspections

Buying or Selling a Home? Get a Level 2 Inspection

A Level 2 chimney inspection is required by NFPA 211 during all real estate transactions. Our certified technicians use video camera technology to examine the flue interior, identify hidden damage, and provide a detailed report you can share with buyers, sellers, or your real estate agent.

Creosote Hazard Assessment

Is Creosote Buildup Putting Your Home at Risk?

Creosote is a highly flammable byproduct of burning wood. Even a thin layer can ignite and cause a devastating chimney fire. Our creosote hazard assessment identifies the type and severity of buildup so we can recommend the safest course of action.

  • ✓ Stage 1–3 creosote classification
  • ✓ Glazed creosote identification
  • ✓ Chimney fire risk evaluation
  • ✓ Professional cleaning recommendations
