Should I use my fireplace if my chimney needs repair?

No, you should avoid using your fireplace if your chimney has damage or structural issues. Using a damaged chimney risks fire hazards, carbon monoxide buildup, and further structural deterioration. If you notice any signs of chimney damage—smoke backup, visible cracks, missing mortar, or damaged flashing—discontinue fireplace use immediately and call Mr. Chimney Sweep for emergency assessment. Our CSIA-certified technicians can quickly determine if your chimney is safe to use and perform necessary repairs. Safety is always our first priority, so when in doubt, have a professional inspect before lighting another fire.

How often should chimneys be inspected to prevent emergencies?

We recommend annual chimney inspections, especially before each heating season, to catch potential problems before they become emergencies. Professional inspections identify creosote buildup, structural issues, blockages, and deterioration early when repairs are less costly and invasive. Combined with regular chimney sweeping, annual inspections are the best preventive measure.
